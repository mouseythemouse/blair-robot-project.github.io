Sponsors

Without our sponsors, parents, and families, we would not be able to compete in FIRST. Not only do they give us the financial means to participate, but they also allow us to work with mentors, use their facilities and resources, and teach us the ways of being professional engineers. Our team is indebted to the kind folks behind each of the following companies.

United Therapeutics Corporation

Founded in 1996 as a means of researching pulmonary arterial disease, United Therapeutics Corporation has developed into the large-scale company it is today. United Therapeutics is a Silver Spring-based biotechnology company focused on the development of innovative medical products that meet the needs of patients with chronic and life-threatening conditions. Currently, United Therapeutics has five products on the market, each of which follow this philosophy. United Therapeutics also has a strong relationship with the community, sponsoring “common good” organizations, biotechnology scholarships, and local organizations and teams like Team 449.

Intelligent Automation Inc.

Headquartered in Rockville, Maryland, IAI has an immense focus on robotics, and has maintained its persona as a diverse "R%D" think tank. IAI’s technologies in the marketplace today include two- and three-dimensional forensics imaging equipment used for matching bullets; platforms, and tools for the development of agent-based systems; perimeter security systems; scheduling systems for complex and dynamically changing environments; tools for fault diagnosis and prognosis in complex systems; ad hoc mobile network protocols; and automated tutoring systems for literacy training. Our relationship with IAI spans from them hosting past Kickoffs to being a past and current employer of some of our team’s mentors.

Privatin Consulting

Also headquartered in Rockville, Privatin Consulting provides assistance to all companies in need of it. It has years of expertise in the consulting field and has helped large organizations, such as the Internal Revenue Service, the U.S. General Services Administration, the Transportation Security Administration, and more. We recently started a relationship with Privatin Consulting and hope to continue it in future years.

National Defense Education Program

The National Defense Education Program is a Department of Defense program that promotes STEM learning for students of all ages. At any school in the country near civilian defense research labs, the National Defense Education Program will encourage students to pursue their interests in STEM. Not only will Department of Defense engineers meet with students in classrooms, but the National Defense Education Program helps sponsor a wide variety of STEM activities students partake in.

Qualcomm

Qualcomm is a large company that is one of the leaders of mobile technology. Ranging from devices such as smartphones to tablets to modems and more, the international team at Qualcomm have made some of the biggest breakthroughs in mobile technology. Qualcomm is also devoted to extending their passion for research to the younger generation and have sponsored various STEM activities for students. At Qualcomm’s headquarters, their makerspace environment called Qualcomm Thinkabit Lab hosts thousands of middle school students daily and encourages their STEM education. Qualcomm has also helped sponsor other STEM initiatives, like The Women Enhancing Technology program.

Maryland Space Business Roundtable

The Maryland Space Business Roundtable (MSBR) is a space industry stakeholder organization comprised of both corporate and individual members located both in Maryland and the surrounding areas. MSBR encourages the growth and development of aerospace-related business in Maryland and focuses on the future of high technology in Maryland. MSBR leads aerospace section of the Governor’s campaign to expand the state’s business base. MSBR encourages students to enter technology-related fields through its robust educational outreach program.

American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS)

The American Association for the Advancement of Science is an international organization devoted to advancing science for the benefit of all people. AAAS has set many broad goals for itself, including enhancing communication, strengthening support for STEM, advancing international scientific cooperation, and fostering STEM education for everyone. Since 1848, AAAS has been successful at achieving these goals, and have recently been able to support organizations as local as school STEM activities.

Takoma Park Folk Festival

The Takoma Park Folk Festival is a long running festival that showcases the artistic talents of many participants, from musicians to craftsmen to artists and more. They have been a long time sponsor of the team, and in return students get the opportunity to volunteer at the festival.

Teamweek

Teamweek is a time-saving online project planner and team calendar that brings the much needed sanity into your workday. It helps you react to change fast, collaborate with your team, manage and share tasks intuitively. Sign up and use it for free for a small team of 5.

TurningPoint Global Solutions

Headquartered in Maryland, TurningPoint Global Solutions is an IT company that specializes in Health IT, software development and integration, cybersecurity, and systems infrastructure support, among many other fields. TurningPoint is one of the fastest growing IT companies in the D.C. area. In 2012, TurningPoint was the winner of the "Technology Firm of the Year" award. This company's services are utilized by a number of large corporations as well as many governmental agencies and departments.
